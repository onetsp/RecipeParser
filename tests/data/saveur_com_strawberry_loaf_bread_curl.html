Strawberry Bread

Saveur test kitchen director Farideh Sadeghin grew up making this fruit-filled recipe with her mother Teresa. Just about any seasonal fruit works; try peaches, blackberries, or mangoes.

INGREDIENTS

Unsalted butter, for greasing pans
3 cups flour, plus more for dusting
1 tsp. baking powder
1 tsp. ground cinnamon
½ tsp. kosher salt
2 cups sugar
1¼ cups canola oil
4 eggs
4 cups roughly chopped strawberries
½ cup strawberry jam (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS

1. Heat oven to 350°. Grease 2 (9"x5" x 2¾") loaf pans with butter and dust with flour; set aside. Whisk flour, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt in a large bowl; set aside.

2. In a separate bowl, whisk sugar, oil, and eggs. Pour wet ingredients over dry ingredients and whisk until just combined. Stir in strawberries and pour batter into prepared pans. If using jam, spoon ¼ cup over each loaf and using a knife, swirl jam into batter. Bake until golden brown and a toothpick inserted in the middle of the loaves comes out clean, about 1 hour. Let cool for 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

Ratings & Reviews (2)

5
by SKUNKWORKS on 2013-06-09

Outstanding! The texture was very light and fluffy, not at all what I expected from a quick bread. Mine took an hour and 20 minutes to bake, and I suspect the "let cool 30 minutes before slicing" means "let cool 30 minutes before removing from pans" as mine were very delicate.

5
by NATTYGIRL34 on 2013-06-25

I had way too many strawberries from our farmer's market and I wanted to make a quick bread like banana bread to use them up and gift to neighbors. This recipe was perfect and I will use it again in the future and I plan to try it with other fruits.

This being an online recipe, I need to confess all the ways I deviated from the recipe-- I used Penzy's cake spice in place of the cinnamon (it's a mild blend of cinnamon, star anise, nutmeg, allspice, ginger, and cloves.) I added some good vanilla extract to the wet ingredients. Instead of putting jam on top, I sprinkled them with vanilla sugar and a pinch of Maldon sea salt. Lastly, I baked them in heart-shaped paper pans that held a little over two cups of batter each for a total of six heart-shaped loaves. I baked them for about 45 minutes in my convection oven. They came out with crackly sweet tops and were moist but light throughout. Great strawberry taste throughout. Great recipe!
